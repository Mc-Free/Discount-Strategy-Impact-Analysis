# Discount Strategy Impact Analysis 
Data-Driven Pricing Evaluation for Eniac (E-commerce Case Study)

 
# Project Summary
In this two weeks project, I worked as a Data Analyst for Eniac, an e-commerce technology company facing a strategic pricing dilemma.
Despite an increase in order volume, the company experienced a decline in total revenue. Based on the company’s recent quarterly results. 
Leadership was divided:

   -	The Marketing Team advocated for aggressive discounting to drive growth.

   - The Board and Investors were concerned that excessive discounts were eroding margins and weakening brand positioning.

This project analyzes internal transactional data to determine whether discounting is a sustainable revenue growth strategy.
 
# 🎯 Business Problem
Eniac needed a data-backed answer to a key strategic question:

  - Do product discounts increase total revenue — or do they simply increase order volume at the expense of profitability?

The outcome of this analysis would directly influence:

   -	Pricing strategy

   -	Brand positioning (premium vs. price-competitive)

   -	Revenue optimization

   -	Long-term growth planning
 
# Analytical Approach
This was not a clean dataset. The internal data required:

-	Cleaning and transforming raw transactional tables

-	Joining orders, order lines, and product data

-	Creating derived metrics such as:

     - Revenue
   
     - Total discount value
   
     - Percentage discount
   
   
-	Time-series aggregation by product category

-	Correlation and trend analysis

-	Multi-faceted visualizations to detect category-specific patterns



# The analysis was conducted entirely in Python, leveraging:

-	Pandas for data transformation 

-	Seaborn & Matplotlib for visualization



 
# 📈 Key Analyses Performed

-	Revenue vs. Discount trends over time

-	Category-level sensitivity to discounting

- Correlation between discount intensity and revenue

-	Volume growth vs. revenue growth comparison

-	Normalized time-series trend comparison

-	Identification of peak alignment and lag effects



 
# Strategic Insights
This project demonstrates how:

 -	Increased order volume does not necessarily translate into increased revenue.

 - Some product categories are highly discount-sensitive, while others are not.

 - Revenue peaks do not always align with discount peaks.

 - Aggressive discounting may create short-term sales spikes but weaken long-term revenue stability.



The findings highlight the importance of data-driven pricing decisions instead of assumption-based strategies.
 
# Business Impact

The analysis provides leadership with:

  - Evidence to evaluate pricing strategy trade-offs.

  - Clear visibility into discount-driven revenue dynamics.

  -	Category-level guidance for optimized discount allocation.

  -	Strategic insight into whether Eniac should compete on price or value.
